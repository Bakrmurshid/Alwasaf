# Alwasaf Design Context

## Design Direction
The page should feel like a formal Saudi customs and logistics partner: precise, operational, responsive, and credible. The design should borrow from the brand logo colors and from the physical world of customs work: documents, routes, ports, fleet movement, and structured follow-up.

## Physical Scene
A procurement manager in a Saudi company opens the page during office hours while a shipment is close to arrival. They need confidence, speed, and a clear contact path, not decoration.

## Color Strategy
Full palette, controlled and formal. Navy carries authority, yellow carries visibility and action, orange adds warmth and operational energy. Use warm neutral backgrounds instead of pure white.

## Color Tokens
- Primary navy: `oklch(35% 0.105 256)`
- Deep navy: `oklch(27% 0.088 256)`
- Brand yellow: `oklch(84% 0.17 86)`
- Brand orange: `oklch(75% 0.16 64)`
- Background: `oklch(97% 0.012 92)`
- Surface: `oklch(99% 0.01 92)`
- Text: `oklch(24% 0.035 255)`
- Muted text: `oklch(48% 0.025 250)`
- Border: `oklch(88% 0.018 250)`

## Typography
Use a readable Arabic-first sans-serif stack:
`"Noto Kufi Arabic", "Tajawal", "Segoe UI", Arial, sans-serif`

English mode can use the same stack for consistency. Use strong hierarchy and avoid thin weights.

## Layout Principles
- Arabic is default and RTL.
- English mode switches to LTR.
- Header must show logo and WhatsApp CTA.
- Hero should immediately show the company category and coverage across Saudi sea, land, and air ports.
- Use structured sections, not nested cards.
- Use larger visual rhythm than a generic corporate template.
- Client logos section must support real logos later without inventing client names.

## Visual Motifs
- Route lines
- Port/fleet/documents visual panel
- Structured process steps
- Navy bands with yellow/orange accents
- Logo-forward brand presence
